Associates Face to Face Day: 18 April 2016

This is the second in the scheduled series of Associates’ Education and Dissemination Training Days (face to face days) for 2016. All NICE Medicines and Prescribing Associates are expected to attend.

Content of the day

We will be covering issues the following issues:
  • Type 2 diabetes: patient decision aid. Following on from the meeting in February, we’ll spend a short time looking at the PDA, its strengths and limitations and how it might be used in practice.
  • The NICE controlled drugs guideline. We know this is high on many Associates’ agendas. We will be joined by Dr Tessa Lewis, GP and GDG chair, and Shelly Patel, from MPP, to discuss the implications for practice and answer your questions on the guideline.
  • Medicines optimisation: examples of Associate work. Associates will be sharing work they have done in relation to NOACs, antimicrobial stewardship and insulin prescribing.
  • Behavioural change. Dr Andrew McDowell, clinical psychologist and Director of Health Coaching, The Performance Coach, will be joining us to talk about this refreshing approach to care.

We recognise that many people like to complete the personal notes form electronically, so this is available here for you to download and bring with you on your Tablet or laptop.

Registration

***Registration is essential as names must be given to both London and Manchester venues***

During registration you will be able to select your preferred location. We also need definite numbers for catering arrangements.

Finally, please remember that the day runs until 4.00 pm. Please book your homeward train journey with that in mind: several of your fellow Associates have noted how disruptive it is if lots of people leave early. We do understand that some people really must leave early, for example for childcare reasons, but please do so discreetly if this applies to you.

If you have any queries please feel free to contact us.
