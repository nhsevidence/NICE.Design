What is META?

The Medtech Early Technical Assessment (META) is a low cost consultancy resource developed by NICE. It offers a gap analysis to help medtech companies understand what evidence they need to make a convincing case to payers about their technology. Facilitating organisations use the tool to advise companies on how to optimise their development plans. While META is designed as a gap analysis tool, it is flexible enough to accommodate many delivery models. Facilitating organisations can set their own model depending on the type of package they want to offer to companies.

Facilitating organisations license the tool from us on an annual basis.

How does META work?

There are 3 stages to using the META tool.

1. Company submission

A medtech company submits details on their product and their high level development plans. This is done by completing an online product synopsis, using the tool. They then choose which facilitating organisation they want to work with.

2. Facilitation

The company attends a face-to-face, video or telephone consultation with their facilitator. As a facilitator, you work through the different sections of the tool with the company and discuss the key issues.

The sections cover a wide range of topics relevant to the product development plans, including:

  • Health Technology Assessment requirements
  • defining and understanding system benefits
  • discussing clinical and economic evidence generation.

    • 3. Report

    After the consultation you write a short report based on the synopsis and on the facilitation session. The report should:

  • reflect what you discussed with the company
  • summarise the evidence requirements and any evidence gaps identified during the consultation
  • highlight areas where the company should do further work to generate evidence of the product’s value to the healthcare system.

    • Once written, another facilitator from your organisation quality assures and authorises it. It is then sent to the company.

    The process takes approximately 4 weeks. But, this can vary depending on how you use the tool and the package you offer.

    Do you provide training for facilitators?

    Yes - we offer a comprehensive training package for every facilitator. It takes you through all the steps needed to use the tool and run consultations, including:

  • detailed reading materials
  • self-assessments
  • a full day of interactive training
  • writing a mock report, and receiving feedback on it
  • a certificate of attendance.

    • We do not accredit facilitating organisations or individuals to use the tool.

    FAQs

    Who can be a facilitator from my organisation?

    All facilitators must meet the requirements in the person specification. We also deliver a comprehensive training package for facilitators. Your staff must complete this before they can start to use the tool.

    When should a medtech company have a META tool consultation?

    The tool can be used at all stages of product development:

  • early engagement – to test assumptions and refine your early development strategy
  • late engagement - to ‘sense check’ the development work that has already been done and determine whether any further work is required.

    • What does the tool do for a facilitator?

    The tool provides a:

  • structure for helping medtech companies develop their evidence base
  • platform for understanding a technology’s clinical development
  • solid understanding of medtech HTA requirements through the training.

    • The tool does not:

  • dictate how detailed your report should be
  • limit the scope of your conversation with a company
  • accredit you to provide advice on behalf of NICE.

    • Does licensing the tool from you mean we are giving advice on behalf of NICE?

    No. While we design and maintain the tool, licensees are not authorised to give advice on our behalf.

    How much does it cost to license the tool?

    There is an introductory offer for organisations looking to license the tool in the first year. Please contact us for further details.

    How can my organisation ensure that companies know it is our report?

    You have the option for your organisation’s branding to appear on both the tool and the report.
